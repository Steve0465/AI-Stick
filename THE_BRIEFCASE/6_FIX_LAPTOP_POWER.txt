║      HOW TO FIX: LAPTOP WON'T STAY ON                      ║
╚════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝


PROBLEM:
────────

Laptop power button lights up for 1 second, then off.
OR
Light comes on, immediately goes off, nothing happens.

Your HP 360s convertible does this.


MOST LIKELY CAUSE:
──────────────────

70% chance: RAM got unseated (easy fix, 5 minutes, $0)
20% chance: Power glitch (super easy fix, 2 minutes, $0)
10% chance: Other hardware (might need pro help)


─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────


TRY THESE FIRST (2 Minutes, Free):
──────────────────────────────────


FIX #1: Power Drain (Fixes 30% of Cases!)
──────────────────────────────────────────

1. Remove battery from laptop
   (Flip over, find release latch or screws)

2. Unplug AC adapter

3. Hold power button for 60 FULL seconds
   (Yes, really. Count to 60.)

4. Plug in AC adapter ONLY (no battery yet)

5. Try to power on

6. If it works:
   → Put battery back in
   → Should stay fixed!

7. If it doesn't work:
   → Try Fix #2


FIX #2: BIOS Reset
──────────────────

1. Plug in AC adapter

2. Hold these 3 keys at the same time:
   Windows Key + V + Power Button

3. Hold for 10 seconds

4. Release

5. Wait 30 seconds

6. Try to power on normally

7. If it works:
   → Fixed!

8. If it doesn't:
   → Try Fix #3 or AI diagnostic


─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────


FIX #3: AI Diagnostic (Best Option)
───────────────────────────────────

Let AI figure out what's wrong:

1. From your Mac or iPhone, connect to Pi:
   ssh pi@aiserver.local

2. Navigate:
   cd /media/pi/AI_STICK

3. Start menu:
   ./START

4. Pick option 5 (Fix laptop won't stay on)

5. Answer the questions:
   - What does it do when you press power?
   - Does screen show anything?
   - Any sounds (fan, beeps)?
   - Battery in or out?
   - What happened before it broke?
   - Is it hot?

6. AI analyzes your answers

7. AI tells you:
   - What's wrong (with % confidence)
   - How to fix it
   - What it'll cost
   - How hard it is

8. Follow AI's instructions


─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────


MOST COMMON FIX: RAM Reseat
────────────────────────────

If power drain didn't work, 70% chance it's RAM.

What You Need:
- Small Phillips screwdriver
- 30 minutes
- YouTube (search "HP 360s RAM access")

Steps:

1. Turn off laptop completely

2. Flip over, remove all bottom screws
   (Check under rubber feet too!)

3. Carefully lift bottom panel
   (Use guitar pick on edges if stuck)

4. Find RAM sticks
   (Usually 2 sticks near center, under WiFi card)

5. Press side clips to release RAM

6. Remove BOTH RAM sticks

7. Try to power on laptop (will beep differently)
   → If beeps changed, RAM was the problem!

8. Put one RAM stick back in

9. Try to power on
   → Works? That stick is good
   → Doesn't work? That stick is bad

10. Try the other stick alone

11. One works and one doesn't?
    → Found the bad RAM!
    → Order replacement (~$30 on Amazon)
    → Use laptop with just the good stick until then

12. Both work alone but not together?
    → Bad RAM slot on motherboard
    → Use just one stick


─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────


HP 360s SPECIFIC ISSUES:
────────────────────────

Your convertible has known problems:

Issue #1: Hinge Flex Damage
────────────────────────────

The 360° hinge can damage the display cable.

Test:
- Try powering on at different angles
- Open to 45°, try power
- Open to 90°, try power
- Open to 180°, try power
- Flip to tablet mode, try power

If it works at ONE angle but not others:
→ Display cable damaged
→ Use it only at that angle (temporary)
→ Or get cable replaced ($100-150 professional)


Issue #2: Overheating Protection
─────────────────────────────────

Thin laptops trigger thermal shutdowns.

Test:
- Put laptop in cool room for 2 hours
- Try to power on immediately
- Works for a bit then shuts off?
→ Thermal issue

Fix:
- Clean fan vents with compressed air
- Open laptop, check fan spins freely
- Might need thermal paste replaced


Issue #3: Battery Circuit
─────────────────────────

Try these combinations:
- AC only (no battery)
- Battery only (no AC)
- Both together

If behavior changes between them:
→ Power delivery problem
→ Try different AC adapter
→ Check charging port (wiggle cable)


─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────


WHEN TO GET PROFESSIONAL HELP:
──────────────────────────────

Stop DIY and call a pro if:

❌ You smell burning
❌ You see physical damage (cracks, burns)
❌ Laptop got wet
❌ Multiple components test bad
❌ You're not comfortable opening it

Also check WARRANTY:
- HP convertibles: 1 year standard
- Check: support.hp.com (enter serial number)
- DON'T open if under warranty!
